KATHERINE DUNHAM

“You know, this isn't theater like it use to be...We weren't pushing Black is beautiful, we just showed it.”

Katherine Mary Dunham directed her own dance company for many years and was successful as a dancer before American and European audiences.

Dunham has been praised as the “matriarch and queen mother of black dance” and deemed an innovator in African-American modern dance.

Always an activist, while a high school student, Dunham opened a private dance school for children of color. Dunham received various supportive awards early in her career which helped finance her extensive travels and study of dance.

She founded and maintained the Katherine Dunham Dance Company and developed the “Dunham Technique” which is still taught today.

She was born on June 22, 1909 in Chicago and died on May 21, 2006 in New York City.

Her official website is: kdcah.org.
