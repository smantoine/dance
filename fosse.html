BOB FOSSE

“I am not an educated person. I didn't come up through a ballet company. I came up through burlesque. So I have a lot of inferiority feelings concerning my own lack of education, my entry into show business. I'm not a Baryshnikov. I'm not a Nureyev. I came up in Vaudeville, Strippers. So I've always had these feelings. But I think they've also helped me.”

Robert Louis Fosse was an American theatrical producer involved in dance, musical theater and film. Fosse won 8 Tony awards for choreography, was nominated for 4 Academy awards. Fosse won Best Director for his work in the movie Cabaret.

Fosse started his career in the movies, influenced by the works Jack Cole, Jerome Robbins and Fred Astaire. He was not classically trained in dance and was considered to have brought new styles to dance as a choreographer, such as “turned in knees,” the “Fosse Amoeba,” shuffling, shoulder movements and what became known as “Jazz Hands.”

Fosse’s best known shows are “Cabaret, Pippin, All that Jazz, Sweet Charity and Chicago." These shows remain iconic.

He was born on June 23, 1927 in Chicago and died September 23, 1987 in Washington D.C.
