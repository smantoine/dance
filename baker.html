JOSEPHINE BAKER

“A violinist had a violin, a painter his palette. All I had was myself. I was the instrument that I must care for...
I ran away from home. I ran away from St. Louis, and then I ran away from the United States of America, because of that terror of discrimination, that horrible beast which paralyzes one's very soul and body.”

Josephine Baker was born in St. Louis Missouri but spent most of her adult professional life in France where she became renowned as of the most recognized performers in the Follies Bergere in Paris. Baker’s personality defined her as a dancer and social activist. Baker emboldened her dance presentation through the strength of her personality

Living in France, Baker actively supported the Civil Rights Movement in the United States. Baker refused to perform before segregated audiences anywhere and her commitment to integrated audiences directly influenced, the desegregation of live entertainment in Los Vegas.

Baker joined Martin Luther King when she spoke at the March on Washington in 1963.

Baker was born on June 3, 1906 and died on April 12, 1975 in Paris.
