ALVIN AILEY

“My dancers must be able to do anything, and I don't care if they are black or white or purple or green. I want to help show my people how beautiful they are. I want to hold up the mirror to my audience that says this is the way people can be, this is how open people can be.”

Alvin Ailey was a choreographer and activist who founded the Alvin Ailey American Dance Theater in New York City in 1958. Appropriately named the “cultural ambassador to the world,” the Alvin Ailey Dance Theater became the premiere platform for people of color.

Ailey created a melange of dance presentations ranging from ballet, jazz and later hip hop. Ailey did not require his dancers to perform a specific technique and, instead asked his dancers to follow his choreography with their best efforts and personal styles.

Ailey was born on January 5, 1931 in Rogers, Texas and died on December 1, 1989 in New York.

To learn more please visit the Alvi Ailey website www.alvinailey.org
