MARTHA GRAHAM

“Great dancers are not great because of their technique, they are great because of their passion.”

Martha Graham an iconic American modern dancer and choreographer developed “The Graham technique.” “The Graham technique” has been credited with revolutionizing American Dance and has served as a positive influence on dancing throughout the world.

Graham abandoned the traditional decorative movements of ballet and created a principle known as “Contraction and Release” which is centered upon the separation of human movement to create either a positive or negative feeling.

The interpretation of “Contraction and Release” could be separately presented depending upon the placement of the body in a weighted and grounded style, in direct opposition to classical ballet’s role of creating an illusion of weightlessness.

Graham lived until 96, performed at the White House and received the “Presidential Medal of Freedom” with distinction.

Graham was born on May 11, 1894 in Pennsylvania and died on April 7, 1991 in New York City.

Her official website is: www.marthagraham.org.
